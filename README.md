Serenity is a **companion** web app designed to help older adults stay independent, healthy, and connected, while giving caregivers peace of mind. 

It focuses on a calm, senior-friendly experience with medication reminders, daily routines, mood check-ins, and lightweight caregiver alerts. 

---

## Product Vision

Serenity supports aging-in-place by combining simple, structured daily guidance with gentle emotional check-ins.
The product aims to reduce missed medications, create predictable routines, and surface early signs of distress to caregivers without feeling clinical or overwhelming.

---

## Key Features

- **Medication reminders**  
  Clear “Next medication” cards with name, dosage, time, and actions like “Took it”, “Snooze”, and “Skip” to mirror real-world decisions. 

- **Daily routine timeline**  
  A seven-step day plan (wake up, meals, walk, rest, wind down) displayed as a simple, scannable list to build healthy habits and predictability.

- **Health and mood check-ins**  
  A lightweight survey asking “How are you feeling today?” with options from “Not well” to “Great” plus an optional free-text note to encourage sharing context.

- **Weekly medication overview**  
  A table view of medications across the week with taken/missed/upcoming states to help caregivers spot patterns and risks at a glance.

- **Conversational companion (Talk to Serenity)**  
  A warm, non-emergency chat-style interaction (“Hello there”) that invites the user to share how they’re doing or pick helpful prompts like “I took my pill” or “Remind me about lunch”. 

- **Safety boundary messaging**  
  Clear, repeated copy that Serenity is not for emergencies, instructing users to call 911 in a crisis, to set appropriate expectations. 

---

## Target Users

- **Primary:** Older adults living at home who benefit from structured routines and simple, reassuring guidance.   
- **Secondary:** Family caregivers and home care staff who need lightweight visibility into medications and wellbeing without complex dashboards. 

---

## Design Principles

- **Calm and reassuring** – Pastel, high-contrast UI with gentle language (“Ready for a gentle check-in whenever you are”) to reduce anxiety. 
- **Cognitive load aware** – Limited choices per screen, clear hierarchy, and plain language labels to support users with mild cognitive decline. 
- **Trust and safety** – Explicit emergency disclaimers and non-clinical tone to avoid overpromising medical capabilities. 

---

## Example User Flow

1. Margaret logs in and sees a personalized greeting (“Good evening, Margaret”) and the next medication reminder. 
2. She confirms she took her medication or snoozes it if needed. 
3. She glances at “Today’s routine” to orient herself to upcoming activities. 
4. She completes a quick mood check-in (“Okay”, “Good”, etc.) and optionally writes a note. 
5. A caregiver later reviews the weekly medication table and check-ins to monitor adherence and wellbeing trends.

## PM Notes (Portfolio Context)

This project showcases end-to-end product thinking for a senior-facing digital assistant:

- Framing the **problem** around missed medications, lack of routine, and caregiver anxiety. 
- Translating constraints of older adults (vision, cognition, tech comfort) into concrete interaction and layout choices.  
- Balancing **functionality** (reminders, logs, check-ins) with a compassionate, non-clinical tone.
- Explicitly defining scope boundaries (not a medical device, not for emergencies) to manage risk and expectations.
